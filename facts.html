Known Facts, Signs & Symptoms

Anorexia (an-o-REK-see-uh) Nervosa

- Involves partial or total abstinence from food (solids or liquids) because one believes any amount of food will cost weight gain

- Often perceives oneself as fat and has an intense fear of gaining weight

- Reflected via an unhealthy preoccupation with food and excercise

- Engages in purging activities such as self induced vomiting and using laxatives

- Anorexia Nervosa can be fatal. The leading cause of death is starvation, while suicide due to the disorder is second in line.

- Signs of one being down with this disorder include extreme restrcted eating, extreme thiness (emaciation), a relentless pursuit of thiness and unwillingness to maintain a normal a normal or healthy weight, intense fear of gaining weight and a distorted body image

Binge Eating Disorder

- Involves excessively bingeing on food

- Includes periods of uncontrolled, impulsive and continous eating till being uncomfortably full

- No compensatory behaviour thereafter (no purging)

- Usually triggered by emotional events and leads victim to turn to food as a means of comfort

- The aftermath involves guilt and self loathing

Bulimia (boo-LEE-me-uh) Nervosa

- Is similar to binge eating, only that Bulimia Nervosa involves eating and purging

- Is normally a repeated cycle of binging and purging

- Is characterized by consuming a large amount of food in comparison to what an individual eats in a short period of time (binge eating) before the guilt of overeating causes the victim to engage in self induced vomiting, extreme excercising, laxative abuse and extreme fasting

Orthorexia Nervosa

- Involves extreme exercise and obsession with eating what is perceived to be healthy

- Reject any food perceived as unhealthy, such as food with oil, butter, or carbohydrates

- Sometimes lead to Anorexia Nervosa, when the victims start to eliminate large number of food from their diet, for the reasons stated above

- Even though they are frequently mixed up, Anorexia is NOT the same as Otheroxia. The main difference is the intention, with the vicitims of Anorexia mainly seeking to lose weight while the victims of Otheroxia mainly seeking to have "clean bodies" by consuming and intaking only healthy foods.
