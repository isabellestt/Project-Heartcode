ADHD

in youths

ADHD is one of the most common neurodevelopmental disorders of childhood.

Hover over the cards below to find out more about the three types of ADHD!



Predominantly Inattentive Presentation



They find it hard to organize or finish a task, pay attention to details, follow instructions or conversations. They are easily distracted or forget details of daily routines.

Predominantly Hyperactive-Impulsive Presentation



They fidget and talk a lot, are restless and have trouble with impulsivity. They may interrupt others a lot, grab things from people, or speak at inappropriate times.

Combined Presentation



They show symptoms from both types of ADHD, and do not really belong in a single category. They may have a hard time realising that they have ADHD.
